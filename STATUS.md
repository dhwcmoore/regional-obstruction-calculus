- (N0) cochain-map naturality / descent-safe subdivision persistence (`rocq/CochainNaturalityDescent.v`).
- Common-subdivision certificate agreement, built on the descent theorem (`rocq/CommonSubdivisionAgreement.v`).
- (E0) exactness reflection (`rocq/ExactnessReflection.v`).
- Soundness of the two proof-carrying certificate forms (`rocq/FirstOrderClassifierCertificate.v`).
- Repeated triple-support incidence condition and Candidate 3b's induced-map theorems (`rocq/RepeatedTripleSupportCandidate3b.v`): the `RepeatedTripleSupport` record, the partial-support impossibility lemma, genuinely-shared-column theorem, and exhibited repairable/non-repairable residue witnesses — checked with `coqchk`.
- Candidate 3b's distinct-support classification, the other half of the same result (`rocq/CandidateThreeBDistinctSupportClassification.v`): under pairwise-distinct triple support (abstracted over any type with decidable equality, no `Point`/finiteness assumption, only the support values need be distinct), no two seams can ever reference the same carrier coordinate, and the induced map is full rank — plus a concrete instantiation confirming this specialises correctly to the cover `candidate_discipline_diagnostic.py` actually uses. Together with the row above, this makes the full Candidate 3b classification (distinct support ⟹ cover-inert; repeated support ⟹ genuinely partial nontrivial quotient) machine-checked at the same level, not diagnostic-only in either direction.
- Refinement witness composition, all three conditions, two and three steps (`rocq/RefinementWitnessComposition.v`, `rocq/RefinementWitnessVerdictComposition.v`, `rocq/RefinementWitnessSequentialComposition.v`): if composed refinement witnesses each satisfy (N0)/(A4)/(E0), their composite does too — `N0_composes`/`N0_composes_three` (associativity of function composition, needs *every* step's own N0), `A4_composes`/`A4_composes_three` (near-definitional: the composite pairing test is literally the *last* step's own pairing test, needs no hypothesis about earlier steps), and `E0_composes`/`E0_composes_three` (a short span-transport argument through linearity, needing *every* step's own (E0), not any step's (N0)). Minimal finite-dimensional vector-space/span infrastructure built from scratch for the E0 proofs. Together, `verdict_safe` composability follows by conjunction. Released as `v0.11-refinement-witness-composition`.
- Disjoint *parallel* refinement witness composition, two branches (`rocq/RefinementWitnessParallelComposition.v`): `N0_parallel_disjoint` and `E0_parallel_disjoint`, built from a genuine direct-sum (product-type) construction rather than function composition — a different construction from every row above, reusing the same `VSpace`/`InSpan`/`IsLinear` infrastructure for E0 plus a new direct-sum constructor and a span-monotonicity lemma. Plus a full A4 classification: `A4_parallel_disjoint_branchwise` (each branch's own A4 survives combination, unconditionally), `A4_parallel_disjoint_nonzero_sum` (the aggregate/scalar pairing test succeeds exactly under an explicit non-cancellation hypothesis, needing neither branch's own A4), and a machine-checked witness (`A4_parallel_aggregate_can_fail_despite_branchwise`, concrete values $+5$/$-5$) that branchwise success does not imply aggregate success. Deliberately does **not** include a bare `A4_parallel_disjoint` theorem — that name belongs to the false naive claim.
- *Coupled* parallel composition's compatibility gate (`rocq/CoupledParallelCompatibility.v`): not full coupled parallel composition, and not any general (N0)/(A4)/(E0) preservation claim about a glued composite — only the well-definedness question underneath Phase 5b's probe. Abstract `Key -> option Value` interface-declaration model (no `Edge`/`Witness` types, no decidable-equality hypothesis): `interface_agreement_allows_glue` (agreement is sufficient, constructively — the glue is exhibited), `interface_disagreement_blocks_glue` and its corollary `incompatible_has_no_glue` (agreement is necessary — an incompatible pair has no glue *at all*, near-definitional once "glue" is stated precisely), and a concrete `Example` (`shared_label_not_sufficient_for_agreement`) upgrading the probe's organic naming-collision finding to `coqchk`-verified. A second section proves `glued_aggregate_vs_naive_sum` (the general single-counting correction between a glued aggregate and the naive disjoint-style sum, by `ring`) and a concrete `Example` (`compatible_glue_can_cancel_aggregate_A4`, grounded in Phase 5d's own computed numbers) that branchwise A4 can hold on both branches while the glued aggregate is exactly zero. No merge/conflict-resolution rule — deliberately not attempted anywhere in this project.
- The conflict-resolution trilemma (`rocq/ConflictResolutionTrilemma.v`): a genuinely separate mathematical question from every row above — not about refinement witnesses or composition at all, but about equality and resolver functions in the abstract. `no_single_value_matches_both_declarations` (the minimal fact: `x <> y -> ~(z = x /\ z = y)`); `full_fidelity_forces_trivial_domain` (a resolver with both full left and right fidelity forces its whole value type to collapse to at most one element); `no_resolver_has_both_fidelities_on_nontrivial_domain` (the operationally meaningful contrapositive — given any two distinct values, no resolver can have both fidelities). Proves *why* a conflict-resolution rule is never neutral; proposes, endorses, or implements none. A second, lossy-vs-structured section shows the impossibility is specifically about single-value (same-type) resolution: `pair_resolver_preserves_both_claims` (a structured resolver preserving both declarations always exists, trivially) and `structure_does_not_exempt_the_resolved_field` (a structured object's own scalar summary field, if it has one, is still fully subject to the same impossibility — structure changes what is preserved alongside a decision, not whether the decision itself can honour both sides).
- The non-lossy lower bound (`rocq/ConflictResolutionLowerBound.v`): how much structure a non-lossy encoding actually requires, given that R11 shows one exists. `nonlossy_encoding_injective` (any encoding recoverable via fixed projections must be injective on `V * V`) and `structured_pair_is_nonlossy` (pairing into `V * V` achieves this bound exactly, no wasted structure). The finite corollary — for `|V| = n`, a non-lossy codomain needs `>= n^2` elements, so no codomain confined to `V` itself can be non-lossy once `n > 1` — is a cardinality restatement of R11's equational impossibility, not a new assumption. Characterises the *shape* a faithful conflict record must have; proposes no concrete schema.
- Bounded conflict-diagnostic completeness (`rocq/ConflictDiagnosticCompleteness.v`, R13): combines R11 and R12 into a closed, four-constructor classification of what a diagnostic about a conflicting shared interface can honestly be — `RefuseDiagnostic`/`ScalarDiagnostic`/`StructuredDiagnostic`/`UnresolvedDiagnostic`. Imports `ConflictResolutionTrilemma.v`/`ConflictResolutionLowerBound.v` directly rather than duplicating them; every theorem is either a direct reuse or a thin corollary. `conflict_diagnostic_classification_total`/`..._exclusive` prove classification into the fragment's four `DiagnosticClass` buckets is total and exclusive; the classes and the underlying constructors are proved pairwise distinct. `no_hidden_neutral_scalar_case` is the headline restatement of R11 in this fragment's vocabulary. Deliberately bounded — see `docs/design/CONFLICT_DIAGNOSTIC_COMPLETENESS.md` §7 for exactly what is not claimed, including that it does *not* claim R11's seven named resolver shapes are an exhaustive taxonomy.
- A typed diagnostic calculus for the R11-R13 fragment (`rocq/TypedDiagnosticCalculus.v`, R14): turns R13's static classification into explicit introduction/elimination/reduction rules over `ConflictDiagnostic V C`. `SoundL`/`SoundR` (left/right-soundness) defined by cases; `structured_intro`/`-left-elim`/`-right-elim` restate R12; `scalar_conflict_loss` restates R11 as an elimination-inadmissibility fact; `refuse_no_composite_left`/`-right` and `unresolved_no_claim_left`/`-right` show neither `RefuseDiagnostic` nor `UnresolvedDiagnostic` has any sound elimination, for two deliberately distinct reasons (proved impossibility vs. absence of computation). `elimination_soundness` is the calculus-level unifying theorem: under conflict, only a `StructuredDiagnostic` can be both left- and right-sound. `RefinesByEvidence` formalises the one reduction rule (`unresolved --> d`); `preservation_under_reduction` and `no_silent_soundness_gain` are its two safety theorems, the second showing that refining `Unresolved` into a `ScalarDiagnostic` under conflict is still fully subject to `scalar_conflict_loss`. Deliberately does *not* derive `REFUSE-NO-COMPOSITE` from `CoupledParallelCompatibility.v`'s glue theorems — a related but distinct object; see `docs/design/TYPED_DIAGNOSTIC_CALCULUS.md` §9-10.

Verified with `make check-rocq` — 18-file dependency closure, `coqchk`-clean. Requires a Rocq/`coqc` toolchain; not assumed available in every environment.

## 2. Exact computational results (Python, exact rational, tested)

- Four-cycle residue classification (`residue_classifier.py`).
- Associator-generated residue, reproducing the same residue from literal associator-field data (`associator_residue.py`, `run_associator_obstruction.py`).
- Repair solver / obstruction language (`repair_solver.py`).
- Refinement witness checks: (A1)-(A4), (N0), (E0), `descent_safe`, `verdict_safe` (`refinement_checker.py`).
- Refinement witness composition search scripts (`refinement_witness_composition_probe.py`, `refinement_witness_a4_e0_counterexample_search.py`, `refinement_witness_composition_boundary_search.py`): computational evidence — ~175,000 cases, 0 counterexamples — that predates and independently corroborates the composition theorems now proved in §1.
- Disjoint parallel composition probe (`refinement_witness_parallel_disjoint_probe.py`, Phase 4b): over 32 cases of two certificate-disjoint refinement witnesses combined by direct sum, (N0) and (E0) always equal AND(branch A, branch B); (A4) does not — 16/32 cases mismatch, always because the combined pairing (a *sum* of the two branches' own pairings, not an AND) cancels to exactly 0 even though both branches individually satisfy their own (A4). This probe result is what motivated the shape of §1's `RefinementWitnessParallelComposition.v` proofs, including the branchwise/aggregate A4 split — no `verdict_safe`-style composite claim is made for the *aggregate* sense of `verdict_safe` without the non-cancellation hypothesis, for the same reason.
- Coupled parallel composition, shared-seam compatibility gate (`refinement_witness_coupled_parallel_probe.py`, Phase 5b): a conservative compatibility gate — no averaging/summing/branch-preference merge rule attempted — deciding whether a glued composite witness is even well-defined. 5 hand-picked cases (not a systematic sweep): 2 consistent-declaration cases glue cleanly and reduce to the disjoint case's own preservation pattern; 3 conflicting-declaration cases (one arising organically between two of this project's own canonical witnesses, not constructed) correctly refuse a composite entirely (`interface_conflict`), never misreported as an (N0)/(A4)/(E0) failure. See `docs/design/REFINEMENT_WITNESS_COMPOSITION_STATUS.md`, Phase 5b, for what this does and does not establish.
- Compatible aggregate-A4 cancellation search (`refinement_witness_coupled_a4_cancellation_probe.py`, Phase 5d): does shared-seam compatibility force non-cancellation of the aggregate (A4)? No — 5 verified cases, all using `INSERT_BRIDGE` (the only canonical witness with a $\geq2$-dimensional cycle space, hence the only one with enough freedom to vary away from the shared seam), where a genuinely `interface_consistent` glue has branchwise A4 preserved on both branches yet the combined aggregate pairing is exactly zero. The disjoint case's branchwise/aggregate split survives fully into the compatible-coupled case, at least for this witness. Not a general theorem — evidence on one witness family, not all possible ones. One instance of this finding is now also `coqchk`-verified independently of the Python machinery — see §1's `CoupledParallelCompatibility.v` row.
- First-order proof-carrying certificate emission and independent checking (`certificate_emitter.py`, `first_order_certificate_checker.py`).
- 1000-case random-residue regression (`tests/test_random_residue_regression.py`).
- Coupled realisability diagnostics: independent generator, shared adjacent-overlap coupling, ordered inclusion-exclusion, Candidate 3b on both distinct- and repeated-support covers.
- Conflict-resolution trilemma classification table (`conflict_resolution_trilemma_probe.py`): checks `docs/design/CONFLICT_RESOLUTION_TRILEMMA.md`'s §4 table over exact rationals — `left_wins`/`right_wins`/`average`/`sum`/`erase` against agreement, idempotence, left/right fidelity, and symmetry. Confirms no named resolver has both fidelities, confirms idempotence and agreement always coincide (not independent), and confirms `sum` sacrifices agreement/idempotence too, not only the fidelities.
- Non-lossy lower bound finite corollary (`conflict_resolution_lower_bound_probe.py`, R12): confirms `pair_resolver` is injective on `V x V` for `|V| = n = 1..6`; confirms `left_wins`/`right_wins`'s image size is exactly `n` (best possible for a codomain-confined resolver) and `erase`'s is exactly `1` (worst), both strictly short of the `n^2` pairs needing distinguishing. Deliberately does not test `sum`/`average`'s image sizes — their outputs are not structurally confined to `V`, so that comparison would be misleading (see the probe's own module docstring).
- Bounded conflict-diagnostic completeness classification (`conflict_diagnostic_completeness_probe.py`, R13): classifies `refuse`/`left_wins`/`right_wins`/`average`/`sum`/`erase`/`pair`/`unresolved` into the fragment's four `DiagnosticClass` buckets, confirms all four buckets are represented and every strategy lands in exactly one, and reconfirms `left_wins`/`right_wins`/`erase`'s lossiness via the R12 pigeonhole argument specialised at codomain `= V`. Deliberately excludes `average`/`sum` from that confinement check, carrying forward R12's own caveat rather than re-deriving it.

Verified with `make check-python` — 181 tests, all passing. This is the default reproducibility path; see [REPRODUCIBILITY.md](REPRODUCIBILITY.md).

## 3. Diagnostic witnesses, not general theorems

These establish that one specific candidate rule does or does not do something on one specific construction — not a theorem about every rule in its class:

- **Independent generator** (`realisability_diagnostic.py`): too free — surjective onto all of `C^1(N;Q)`.
- **Shared adjacent-overlap coupling** (`coupled_realisability_diagnostic.py`): rank drops but the image collapses entirely into `im(delta^0)` — cohomological collapse.
- **Boolean proper-crossing diagnostic** (`boolean_crossing_diagnostic.py`): a deterministic, parameter-free rule that produces a non-degenerate residue outside `im(delta^0)` on one specific cover. Non-linear — no rank or quotient to compute.
- **Ordered inclusion-exclusion** (`lattice_ie_diagnostic.py`): globally-indexed but the associator formula cancels exactly the shared terms — full rank, disguised independence.
- **Candidate 3b, distinct-support cover** (`candidate_discipline_diagnostic.py`, `rocq/CandidateThreeBDistinctSupportClassification.v`): cover-inert — the cover never lets two triple overlaps coincide, so nothing can be shared. Now machine-checked (§1) as a general fact about any pairwise-distinct-support assignment, not just the one concrete cover the diagnostic ran.
- **Candidate 3b, repeated-support cover** (`repeated_triple_support_diagnostic.py`, `rocq/RepeatedTripleSupportCandidate3b.v`): the first positive linear/rational witness — `rank(B)=2`, neither full rank nor coboundary collapse. Also machine-checked in Rocq, which upgrades the *incidence condition and the induced-map facts* to proved status (§1) while the underlying claim — that this is a structurally meaningful or unique coupling discipline — remains diagnostic.

## 4. Not claimed

This repository does not claim:

- full presentation invariance (comparing two different coarse presentations of the same regional situation and concluding their obstruction verdicts agree, in general);
- a fully verified end-to-end implementation (the Rocq proofs are abstract or instantiated on the paper's own concrete numbers; they do not mechanise `finite_algebra.py` or `regional_composition.py` themselves — see each `rocq/*.v` file's header comment for its exact scope);
- that every associator residue is structurally meaningful — the independent generator (§3) is a counterexample to that on its own;
- that every coupled generator produces a nontrivial obstruction quotient — most tried so far do not (§3);
- that Candidate 3b is the final, unique, or general coupling principle — it is one rule, verified on one family of covers.
- that disjoint parallel composition preserves the *aggregate* (scalar) (A4) in general — §1's `A4_parallel_aggregate_can_fail_despite_branchwise` is a machine-checked witness where two certificate-disjoint branches each individually satisfy (A4) but their direct-sum composite's aggregate pairing cancels to exactly zero (branchwise preservation, proved unconditionally in §1, is a genuinely different and weaker-to-state, stronger-to-hold claim than the aggregate);
- that coupled parallel composition preserves anything **in general** — no preservation candidate exists for it, in either this repository or `veribound-fce`'s design documentation. §2's shared-seam probe is evidence on 2 hand-picked *consistent-declaration* cases only, not a theorem, and says nothing at all about the *conflicting*-declaration case beyond "no composite exists to test." §1's `CoupledParallelCompatibility.v` proves exactly that well-definedness fact — agreement is necessary and sufficient for a glue to exist — and nothing about (N0)/(A4)/(E0) preservation of one;
- that a conflicting shared seam has any principled resolution — no merge, averaging, or branch-preference rule is proposed anywhere in this project; conflicting declarations are diagnosed (`interface_conflict`), not resolved;
- that compatible aggregate-A4 cancellation is *common*, *typical*, or *inevitable* — §2's Phase 5d found and verified that it is *possible*, on one witness family, not a statement about frequency across any wider class of witnesses this project has not built;
- that any conflict-resolution rule is correct, recommended, or endorsed — §1's `ConflictResolutionTrilemma.v` and §2's probe classify seven candidate resolver shapes by what each sacrifices; none is chosen, and §4 of the design doc states explicitly that `external_authority` is not even the same *kind* of object the other six are;
- that §1's non-lossy lower bound (`ConflictResolutionLowerBound.v`) specifies a concrete diagnostic schema — it characterises the abstract minimum (injectivity on `V * V`), not field names, types, or serialisation formats. No such schema is designed in either repository.
- that R13's bounded completeness (`ConflictDiagnosticCompleteness.v`) proves completeness for all possible fusion, policy, or coupled-composition systems — it proves completeness for one small, explicitly bounded four-constructor fragment, chosen to match this project's own proved results and `veribound-fce`'s own applied vocabulary. A real system with, say, a partial-recovery diagnostic would need its own fragment;
- that R13 chooses a resolver, solves general coupled parallel composition, handles policy authority/downstream fusion/restriction/failure composition, or wires anything into `fce_check.py` — see `docs/design/CONFLICT_DIAGNOSTIC_COMPLETENESS.md` §7 for the complete list;
- that R13's four-class completeness contradicts `CONFLICT_RESOLUTION_TRILEMMA.md`'s own §10 disclaimer that R11's seven named resolver *shapes* are not exhaustive. They are different, non-contradicting claims at different levels: R13 classifies structural *shapes* of diagnostic (four of them, closed by the Coq type), not an exhaustive enumeration of every function that could populate the scalar or structured class.
- that R14's `REFUSE-NO-COMPOSITE` is derived from `CoupledParallelCompatibility.v`'s `interface_disagreement_blocks_glue`/`interface_agreement_allows_glue`. It is not — that theorem concerns a specific `Key -> option Value` glue construction, a related but distinct object from `ConflictDiagnostic V C`. R14 grounds the rule only in R13's own already-stated correspondence between `RefuseDiagnostic` and `interface_conflict`, not in a new derivation from the glue theorems;
- that R14 models time, concurrency, or multiple rounds of diagnostic refinement — `RefinesByEvidence` is a single, one-shot `unresolved --> d` transition, not a general revision calculus, and nothing in R14 says when (or whether) evidence for an unresolved case ever arrives;
- that R14 commits `veribound-fce` to a stateful diagnostic-refinement engine. `NonLossyConflictDiagnostic` and `conflict_diagnostic_classifier.py` remain exactly what they are — static schema and classifier. Whether R14's reduction rule ever gets an applied counterpart is a separate, unstarted question.
